Statement

The core of my work is soul.

I don’t create VRChat worlds and avatars just to make something that looks impressive — I create them to make something that feels real.

A world should hold atmosphere even when nobody is inside it. An avatar should carry presence even without words.

I build with intention: the shape of a space, the rhythm of its layout, the quiet corners, the loud spots — the way materials contrast, the way a broken screen can still feel like it’s transmitting something.

My style leans toward industrial dreamcore: concrete, cables, rails, drifting fragments — but always with warmth hidden inside.

Everything I make is built from scratch. Not because assets are wrong, but because my process is personal. I want to know that what you see came directly from my hands.

Every vertex placed with intention. Every poly polished with love.

In the end, I want people to remember one thing: not the graphics, not the complexity — but the feeling.

If someone returns to one of my worlds years later and still feels something, then I built it right.
